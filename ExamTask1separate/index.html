A quick peek at rhinos

Welcome to this site about rhinos! The actual name is rhinoceres, but rhino is more frequently used. Rhino are the second largest land animals after elephants, but they are not as popular or known as them, so this site will hopefully help people find out more about rhinos and the challenges they face.

There are five rhino species: White, Black, Indian, Sumatran and Javan. Most of the species have subspecies which tend to be diffrentiate. The species themselves have characteristics with which they can be distinguished, such as size or number of horns.

Many people think of all rhinos having two horns, or that all rhinos come from Africa, while some people might have heard about the Indian rhino. The Javan and Sumatran rhino are virtually unknown, and this is due making up only 0.5% of the total rhino population combined, as seen in the figure below.

The Sumatran rhino is only seen in captivity in one place outside Indonesia and Malaysia, and the Javan rhino is not seen in captivity currently, so it is understandable why they are unknown. Many people might also not be able to see the differences between the rhino species. Hopefully the table and the graphic on this page will help a bit.
